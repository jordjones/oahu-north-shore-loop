V2 route logic: added the two inland options: Waimea Valley Road and Pupukea Road / Kaunala Trail gate. Puʻu O Mahuka remains the best drive-up high-view stop. Kaunala is not a drive-through viewpoint — DLNR says vehicles stop at the gated reserve, then hiking/mountain-bike only.
Best version for your remaining afternoon

Keep Waimea Bay + Puʻu O Mahuka as the priority. If you want a quick inland taste, add Pupukea Road to the gate and turn around. If you want a real walk, choose either Waimea Valley or Kaunala Trail — not both — unless you are okay rushing Matsumoto/return.

BMW / road safety notes

Paved roads are fine: Kamehameha Hwy, Waimea Valley Road, Puʻu O Mahuka Road, Pupukea Road. Do not drive past gates, onto reserve roads, or onto dirt tracks. Peacock Flats/Kuaokalā vehicle access remains a no-go today.

8

Pūpūkea Road → Kaunala Trail gate

Optional 30–90 min · Second inland road / hike option

The second inland road: drive up Pupukea Road until the road ends near the gated reserve/Camp Pupukea area. DLNR says public vehicles are not allowed inside the reserve; Kaunala Trail is hiking/mountain-bike only, weekends/holidays, with legal roadside/neighborhood parking below the Boy Scout camp. Good quick leg-stretch; do not take the BMW past gates/dirt-road nonsense.

Parking: Park legally below/near Boy Scout camp; no valuables

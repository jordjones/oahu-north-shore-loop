Best fit: if you want legal, scenic, rural, and not generic-touristy, use the west/northwest loop: Puʻu O Hulu → Mauna Lahilahi → Keālia/Mokulēʻia. It keeps the Kaʻala-adjacent feel without forcing closed roads.
Do not drive the Kaʻala / Peacock Flats / Kuaokalā roads right now: DLNR reports Mokulēʻia Access Road closed from storm damage and Space Force gate/Satellite Tracking Station Road access closed. Mt. Kaʻala vehicle access is controlled government road.
How to choose

Normal car / today: main scenic route. Best high-country feel: Poamoho only if you confirm permit, road condition, and vehicle suitability. Closest legal Kaʻala-adjacent hike: Keālia Trail, but you gain elevation on foot.

Why this changed from v2

v2 used Puʻu O Mahuka as the clean paved high-point. You pushed correctly: it is scenic but more touristy and less rural/high-country. v3 moves the plan west/northwest and treats Poamoho as the better elevated-rural alternative, while keeping DLNR closures visible on the map.
